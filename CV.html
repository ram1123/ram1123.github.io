    Research & Experience

    Physics Analysis | aQGC measurement with W+W-

    I am working on the measurement of the aQGC with opposite-sign WW in semi-leptonic decay in association with two jets with 2016 data collected at 13 TeV having an integrated luminosity of 35.9 $fb^{-1}$. The events are selected by requiring two jets with large rapidity separation and dijet mass, one or two lepton (electrons or muons), and a W or Z boson decaying hadronically. The hadronically decaying $W/Z$ boson is reconstructed as one large radius jet. The constrains on the quartic vector boson interactions in the framework of dimension-eight effective field theory operators are reported. Currently, the analysis is approved and we are moving towards publication soon.

    Monte-Carlo Generation

    Experience with the MadGraph generator for the generation of polarized WW scattering sample along with the anomalous Quartic Gauge coupling sample generation using the reweight feature provided by the MadGraph generator. The reweight feature allows one to save scan over different parameters of a model and save all of them as weight in single sample so that one has to just save n number of weights instead of saving n-different samples. This allows to save the parking space and the computing resources.

    Full detector simulation: Have experience in the generation of the full detector simulation, starting from gridpack to the MiniAOD, using the cmsDriver package provided by the CMS analysis software, known as CMSSW.

    Hardware | Gas Electron Multiplier (GEM Detector) | twiki link

    • I was involved with the CMS Gas Electron Multiplier (GEM) collaboration. GEM is a micro-patterned gaseous detector. This detector has excellent space resolution ($\approx 100\mu m$), time resolution ($\approx 4-5ns$) and it is also radiation hard. CMS detector, which is one of multi-purpose detectors at CERN LHC, will be exposed to high radiation in endcap region ($1.6 < |\eta | < 2.4$) after long shutdown-2 (2019-2020). To restore the redundancy of the muon system, GEM detector is planned to be installed during Long shutdown-2, and CMS collaboration has also approved it. This project is named as CMS GEM GE1/1 upgrade.
    • I was mainly involved with the beam test of the GE1/1 detectors as it was needed to test detector's performance with real beam. We tested our GEM detectors using muon beam which is coming from Super-Proton-Synchrotron at CERN. For this, I have been one of the active shifters. Apart from this I have also improved software for reading the raw data and implemented the alignment algorithm which aligns and gives space resolution along with its other basic parameters like beam profile, its correlation, cluster size, and so on.
    • On the hardware side, I have contributed to lab set-up at University of Delhi and performed the assembly and basic characterization to study the GEM detectors.
    • I served as convener of the ``GEM Detector Response Modelling Meeting'' subgroup of GEM-DPG from July 2015-November 2016 and a contact person for ``GEM Phase 2 R \& D'', DPG group from September 2015-November 2016.
